So What?

According to the Consumer Electronics Association, in 2012 global spending on electronics is expected to surpass US$1 trillion. As use of these electronics increases around the world, the question of how to properly manage them when consumers are finished with them becomes more urgent. Unfortunately, not enough of our electronics are re-used, recycled or refurbished. Too many of them end up directly in landfills or recovered in an unsafe manner. According to the UN Environmental Programme, some 20 to 50 million metric tons of e-waste are generated worldwide every year, with mobile phones and televisions contributing 10 million tons per year by 2015. According to the Environmental Protection Agency, only 12.5% of electronic waste is being recycled worldwide.
