What is E-waste?

E-waste is a popular, informal name for electronic products nearing the end of their "useful life." Computers, televisions, VCRs, stereos, copiers, and fax machines are common electronic products. Many of these products can be reused, refurbished, or recycled.

The number of e-waste in Australia is predicted to be approximately 700 million items in 2020 if there is no e-waste recycling program. Australia is a country that is left behind when it comes to e-waste legislation. According to the current law, it is the responsibility of consumers to recycle e-waste since the government has no funding to finance the recycling. It led consumers to leave the e-waste not recycled and throw it out on landfills.
